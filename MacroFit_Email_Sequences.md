### Email 2 — How the 6-Month Cycle Works
**Timing:** ~5 days before start date  
**Subject:** Only 5 days until you start!

Only 5 days until you start! (for most of you)

In the meantime, I'll explain a bit more in depth how Macrofit works.

Macrofit is designed to go through a 6-month cycle — yes, you read that correctly. To get optimal results, I encourage you to stick to it because your calories are being cycled over a 6-month period of time. This is done because your Macro Points are tweaked weekly based on your progress and adherence.

While your Macro Points are worked down and your weight decreases, it's incredibly important for your Macro Points to eventually be worked back up while your weight stays down — this is called a reverse diet. This is 16 weeks of cycling calories down plus 8 weeks of cycling calories up (reverse diet). This is 24 weeks or 6 months!

It is CRITICAL to your long-term success because your calories cannot just go down endlessly. They must be brought back up to diet back down again. Most Macrofitters don't notice the process and continue to lose weight for the majority of the reverse diet. This helps to ensure that your metabolism is in a great place and is taken care of long term. This is also how people can stay on Macrofit for years! (Not that you'll need to — we just have a lot of people that love the convenience and ease of Macrofit and want to stick around long term.)

P.S. We're here to support you! If you have any questions, email us at info@macrofit.com

### Email 3 — Cooking for Your Family
**Timing:** ~4 days before start date  
**Subject:** Yes, you can cook MacroFit for your whole family

4 MORE DAYS UNTIL YOUR JOURNEY BEGINS! (for most of you)

Yes, you can cook your Macrofit meals for your family too! I figured this info would be helpful before you grocery shop for next week.

**Sidenote:** Every MacroFit recipe is designed for 7 servings. Eyeballing your portion only works when you are cooking for yourself, since the 7 servings will average out across the week. But when you are doubling and tripling recipes for family, do not eyeball serving sizes.

Remember, Macrofit only works by you staying in a strategic calorie deficit. ANYTHING extra (other than your cheat meal) will put you in calorie surplus. The serving size 100 PERCENT matters!

**How to Do MacroFit While Feeding Your Family**

Easy! Just buy 2x the ingredients and double the recipe. Set aside your half of the dish and add to 7 meal prep containers and let the family eat the other half!

1. **Double the Recipes** — Each MacroFit recipe makes 7 servings. By doubling it, you get 14 servings — 7 for you and 7 for your family. That way, you're not cooking two separate meals.
2. **Weigh Empty Pot Before You Cook** — Use a food scale to weigh your pot or pan in grams before adding anything. Write that number down.
3. **Weigh the Pot with Contents After Cooking** — Once the food is done, weigh the entire pot with the cooked food, then subtract the original pot weight. That gives you the total weight of just the food.
4. **Divide by 14** — Take that total food weight and divide it by 14 servings. That number is how many grams = one serving.
5. **Portion Your Meals** — Scoop 7 servings into meal prep containers for yourself (using your gram measurement), and let your family eat the rest.

**FAQ: Can my husband/wife/family members eat what I specifically eat?**
No. The combination of your meals, snacks, and restaurant options are meant for you and you only. We assign a VERY specific Macro Point budget to you that is adjusted based off your progress, age, height, weight, gender, activity level, measurements, and adherence to calculate weekly Macro Point allotments for each Macrofitter. But with that being said, the whole family will definitely benefit by having healthier meals to eat at the house. They just aren't specifically calculated for their calorie deficit.

### Email 4 — Full How-It-Works Breakdown
**Timing:** ~3 days before start date  
**Subject:** 3 Days Until You Begin

**STARTING MACROFIT**

First, we look at your questionnaire and starting data to determine your Macro Point allotment. Macro Points are how we measure energy coming from food. Think of this as a weekly budget to build your meal plan. As you plan for each week you choose the recipes and snacks that you like and that work for your macro point budget. Your budget changes based on progress and adherence!

**SNACKS**
You will first select 7 snack groups. (Remember you are planning for 7 days at a time.) You could technically select 7 different groups but that can get pricey and confusing. I recommend selecting 2 snack groups — one group for 4 days and the other for 3 days.

Each day you eat a snack group. Most snack groups are made up of 2–3 items. For example, if you select the snack group (Premier Protein Shake, Banana, and Blue Bell Ice Cream), you will eat all 3 of these items in 1 day.

**MEALS**
After selecting 7 snack groups for next week's meal plan, you would then select meals. Select a breakfast, lunch, and dinner to prep for next week. All recipes are made for 7 servings. You will notice as you select meals your macro point budget goes down. Ideally you want to end up with zero macro points left over. I like to use the "fill my macro points" feature — it removes the guesswork and only shows options that work for my macro budget.

After picking your 3 courses (breakfast, lunch, and dinner) you will then submit your meals to access your grocery list. Your grocery list will show items needed for snacks and all items needed for your 3 main preps. We even have it broken down by areas in the grocery store!

**MEAL PREP SUBS**
If you get tired of eating prep, you can easily change it up! Just go to your Meals tab in your dashboard and you'll see an alternative, single serving meal, and on the go option!

- ⭐ Alternative meals — normal 7-serving recipes
- ⭐ Single Serving meals — simple recipes like ground meat and rice or sandwiches made for 1 serving
- ⭐ On the Go — restaurant meals where we tell you exactly how to order to keep macros on track!

**WORKOUTS**
Try to do 3 workouts a week! I don't care what you do, as long as you are moving then you are doing good. You'll have access to the workout library when your start date begins. If you already have your own workout regimen, then great!

Steps — if you track your steps, aim for a minimum of 8,000 a day.

**SUMMARY**
Order and timing of eating meals doesn't matter one bit. Just try to eat all the food each day! Week 1 is the absolute most food you'll eat on the program and it's still a calorie deficit. Each week after that, small adjustments will be made to your macro point allotment as long as you are adherent to the program! I know it's a lot at first, but I promise it's worth it! I can't wait to see what you accomplish!

If you have any questions, feel free to reach out to us at info@macrofit.com

### Email 6 — Expectations: Fat Loss vs. Weight Loss
**Timing:** ~Week 1–2  
**Subject:** Will I lose 20 lbs in a month?

Will I lose 20 lbs in a month?

The answer is: highly unlikely. Everyone, repeat after me: LOSING ONE POUND OF BODY WEIGHT PER WEEK ON AVERAGE IS AN EXCELLENT AND SUSTAINABLE RATE OF LOSS!

This was not the regularly scheduled email that I intended to send today, but I met a sweet lady at a basketball game this weekend that told me she was doing Macrofit and that she had only lost 3–4 lbs. She told me that she's about 4 weeks into her Macrofit journey. She admitted this like it was a problem, then let me know she might have had a few extra cheat meals. My mind was BLOWN that she thought this was slow progress, but I had to step back and check myself and explain to her that that is an excellent rate of loss, especially with extra added calories from more than 1 cheat meal per week.

When you are doing a program like Macrofit, where keeping muscle is prioritized (this is why you're eating plenty of protein), you are not going to be shedding nearly as much weight from muscle/water as you would with most diets. This is a FAT LOSS PROGRAM, not a weight loss program. And there is absolutely a difference between those two things. Yes, the majority of people will lose weight from losing body fat, but my goal is not to get you down to the lightest version of yourself. My goal is to help you lower your body fat percentage while keeping your muscle — because the more muscle you have, the more calories you burn just living (plus SO MANY other benefits) — so you definitely don't want to lose that muscle tissue!

I don't want anyone shortchanging their results because they're lacking the appropriate expectations, especially when their results are spectacular!

Please also be aware that a MULTITUDE of factors go into your rate of loss: previous dieting history (this is a big one if you've been a yo-yo dieter), how much body fat you have to lose (the more you have, the easier it is to lose — the less, the slower: typically! This is not true for everyone), age, activity level, gender, hormones, hypothyroidism or insulin resistance — you WILL still lose with these things — but it will likely be a little longer process.

I highly recommend buckling into Macrofit for AT LEAST 24 weeks, because that's how long it takes to complete one cycle of dieting and reverse dieting, doubling that is even better! But it allows for your metabolism to be well tended to while you're aiming to lose body fat.

Y'all are amazing and I'm so thankful that I get to be a part of your journey!

P.S. We're here to support you! If you have any questions, email us at info@macrofit.com

### Email 7 — Adherence
**Timing:** ~Week 2  
**Subject:** Your adherence matters more than you think

Every single macro you eat is accounted for! This is why anything eaten "off plan" may put you in a calorie surplus. Macrofit is designed to keep you in a cycled calorie deficit. A calorie deficit is where you are burning more calories than you are consuming. That's it! And when this happens you begin to burn energy from fat stores — you know, BURNING FAT! 🙌

With Macrofit, I want your goal to be to AVERAGE 8+ on adherence! Why? Because that is how you get results!

I go through the reasons people cancel their membership and sometimes I see "not having results," but I go look at their chart and their average adherence is a 4. Well of course they're not having results! No matter what fat loss lifestyle you choose to do, if your adherence is a 4/10, you're not going to have results.

There will be weeks where adherence is low — holiday, vacation, emergency, etc — those things are part of life and expected. But a couple of weeks of low adherence won't undo all the weeks of 9–10 level adherence!

I urge you to go to the progress section of your account and add up all your weeks of adherence and divide it by the number of weeks listed on the chart to see what your average is! Then just work to consistently get above that number and get your adherence to where you're averaging over 8!

I KNOW YOU CAN DO IT! 💪
