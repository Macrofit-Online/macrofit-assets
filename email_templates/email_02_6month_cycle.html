Hi {{contact.first_name}} — MacroFit is built around a 6-month cycle, and that's completely intentional. Here's why it works.

Your Macro Points are adjusted weekly based on your progress and adherence. Over the first 16 weeks, your points are gradually worked down as your body loses fat. Then over the next 8 weeks, your points are worked back up — this is called a reverse diet.

That's 24 weeks. 6 months. One complete cycle.

Phase 1

Calorie Cycling Down

16 weeks — Macro Points are strategically reduced week by week as your body loses fat.

  

Phase 2

Reverse Diet

8 weeks — Calories are brought back up to protect your metabolism and lock in your results.

Why does this matter?

Your calories can't just go down forever. If they did, your metabolism would eventually adapt and progress would stall. The reverse diet phase brings your calories back up strategically — so your metabolism stays healthy, your results hold, and you're set up to keep going.

Here's the part most people don't expect: most MacroFitters continue to lose fat during the reverse diet phase. Your body is still in a healthy deficit — it's just being managed smarter.

This is also why so many members stay on MacroFit for years. Not because they have to — but because the structure works and the convenience becomes something they don't want to give up.
