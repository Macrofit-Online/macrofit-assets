The key thing to know first

Every MacroFit recipe is designed for 7 servings. When you're cooking just for yourself, eyeballing your portion works fine — the 7 servings average out across the week. But when you're doubling a recipe for family, serving size matters exactly.

MacroFit only works when you stay in a strategic calorie deficit. Anything extra puts you in a surplus — so your portion has to be precise.

How to do it

1

Double the recipe

Each MacroFit recipe makes 7 servings. Double it to get 14 — 7 for you, 7 for your family. One cook, one meal.
2

Weigh your empty pot before you cook

Use a food scale and write the number down.
3

Weigh the pot again after cooking

Subtract the pot weight to get the total weight of just the food.
4

Divide by 14

That number is your gram weight per serving.
5

Portion your 7 servings into meal prep containers

Let the family eat the rest.

FAQ

Can my family follow my exact MacroFit plan?

No — your Macro Point budget is calculated specifically for you based on your age, height, weight, gender, activity level, and progress. But your family will absolutely benefit from eating healthier, more balanced meals. They just aren't in your calorie deficit.
