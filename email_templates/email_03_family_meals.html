4 Days Until You Start

Yes, you can cook MacroFit for your whole family.

This is one of the most common questions we get — and the answer is yes. You don't have to cook two separate meals. Here's exactly how to do it.

Important: Every MacroFit recipe is designed for 7 servings. When cooking for your family, do not eyeball your portion — especially when doubling or tripling recipes. The serving size matters because MacroFit works by keeping you in a strategic calorie deficit. Anything extra will put you in a surplus.

How to cook MacroFit meals for the whole family:

  • 1
    Double the Recipe Each MacroFit recipe makes 7 servings. Double it to get 14 — 7 for you, 7 for your family. One meal, everyone eats.
  • 2
    Weigh Your Empty Pot Before Cooking Use a food scale to weigh your pot or pan in grams before adding anything. Write that number down.
  • 3
    Weigh the Pot After Cooking Once the food is done, weigh the pot with the cooked food, then subtract the original pot weight. That gives you the total weight of just the food.
  • 4
    Divide by 14 Take that total food weight and divide by 14 servings. That number is how many grams = one serving for you.
  • 5
    Portion Your Meals Scoop your 7 servings into meal prep containers using your gram measurement. Let your family eat the rest.

Can my husband / family members eat exactly what I eat?

Not exactly — your Macro Points budget is personalized to you based on your age, height, weight, gender, activity level, measurements, and adherence. Your family will absolutely benefit from eating the same healthy meals, but they aren't calculated for their specific calorie deficit. Your portion is yours.
