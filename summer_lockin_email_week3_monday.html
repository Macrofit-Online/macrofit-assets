Week 3 is where most people quietly stop. Not because the plan stopped working. Not because they're incapable. But because a few things creep in that feel bigger than they actually are — and I want to address them head on before they get to you.

Being too hard on yourself

You are learning a new way of eating and a new relationship with food. That takes time. You are not going to be perfect at it in Week 3. Nobody is.

The goal is not perfection — it's progress. If you had a rough day, the next meal is a fresh start. Give yourself the same grace you would give a friend who was trying something new. You deserve that too.

All-or-nothing thinking

This is the one that takes more people out than anything else.

One off-plan meal becomes "I already ruined it" — and suddenly the whole week is gone. Here's the truth: one meal does not derail 42 days. One decision to quit does.

You can have a hard day and still have a great week. You can have a hard week and still finish this challenge. It is never all or nothing.

Unrealistic expectations

If you're waiting to look like a completely different person by Week 3, MacroFit is going to feel like it's not working — because it was never built for that.

MacroFit is built for lasting change. Real change. The kind that sticks six months from now, a year from now, five years from now. That kind of change doesn't happen in three weeks. It builds. And right now, in Week 3, it's building — even when you can't see it yet.

Lack of support

This one is on us — and it's exactly why the Summer Lock In exists.

You are not doing this alone. You have a community of people in the FB group who are in the exact same place you are right now. You have a weekly video message from Chelsea covering exactly what you need to hear. You have the app tracking your progress every single day.

Use it. Post in the group. Submit a question through the app. Let the community carry some of the weight this week. That's what we're here for.
